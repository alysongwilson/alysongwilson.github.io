ABSTRACT

We would like to commend the authors on writing a thought-provoking article that provides new methodology and metrics for assessing the current reliability of discrete-use systems. Complex systems can typically fail for many reasons, and having tools to understand these competing failure modes as well as the proportion likely identified can help with understanding of the health and growth potential of the system.

We will focus our comments in three areas:
  1. The assumptions of the methodology and how these might impact the interpretation of results.
  2. Passive versus active management goals.
  3. Suggestions for alternate measures and metrics that might be considered to complement those proposed by Hall, Ellner, and Mosleh.
