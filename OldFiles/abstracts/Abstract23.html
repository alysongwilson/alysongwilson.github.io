ABSTRACT

This article examines the risk of surveillance sampling rates from three perspectives: detection probability of defects in a portion of a population using pass/fail data, detection of a trend in a defective portion of the population using pass/fail data, and detection of a trend using quantitative degradation measurements. Understanding these risks will help decision makers choose a sampling rate when surveilling a population to discover manufacturing defects and deterioration.
