ABSTRACT

In this paper we expand on recent advances in Bayesian inference for multilevel data in fault trees and Bayesian networks. As a first example, we compare the Bayesian fault tree and incomplete data approaches to statistical inference for multilevel data in fault trees. As a second example, we consider two a priori representations of uncertainty about the parameters of a Bayesian network: A multinomial-Dirichlet model and an extension of the imprecise Dirichlet model. We calculate the a posteriori uncertainty after updating with data using Markov chain Monte Carlo and compare the results.
