Introduction to Bayesian Thinking

The learning objectives for this topic:
  • Define: prior distribution, likelihood, posterior distribution
  • Find the posterior mean, posterior median, posterior variance, posterior standard deviation, central credible interval, highest posterior density interval
  • Explain and apply Bayes' Theorem: "posterior is proportional to prior times likelihood:
  • Define: reference prior, noninformative prior, informative prior, conjugate prior
  • Apply Jeffreys' Rule to derive prior distributions
Reading: Hoff, Chapters 1-3; Albert, Chapters 1-3
