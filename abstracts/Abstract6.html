ABSTRACT

Recent advances in computational capabilities often make engineering simulations of lifetime tractable. We consider the case in which there exist lifetime data from a computational model as well as data from a physical reliability experiment. In addition, there may exist one or more expert opinions about the expected lifetime for selected factor settings. We simultaneously analyze the combined data using a hierarchical Bayes model. In this integrated approach we recognize important differences, such as possible biases, in these experimental data and expert opinions.
